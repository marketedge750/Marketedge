Full Broker Reviews

Fidelity Investments

★★★★★
4.9 / 5 — Best Overall
Fees$0
Min. Deposit$0
Fractional SharesYes

Fidelity remains our top-ranked broker in 2026 for one simple reason: it wins on almost every dimension that matters — zero commissions, exceptional research tools, top-tier customer service, and an educational library that rivals dedicated learning platforms.

The platform introduced fractional shares on all S&P 500 stocks, making it easier than ever for new investors to build diversified portfolios with limited capital. Their ZERO expense ratio index funds remain a standout for cost-conscious long-term investors.

Pros

  • No commission on stocks, ETFs, and options
  • Outstanding research and screeners
  • Excellent retirement account support (IRA, Roth IRA)
  • 24/7 customer service with real humans

Cons

  • Desktop platform can feel dated for active traders
  • No cryptocurrency trading (yet)
Verdict: The safest choice for the vast majority of investors. Especially strong for long-term, buy-and-hold strategies and retirement planning.

Charles Schwab

★★★★★
4.8 / 5 — Best for Beginners
Fees$0
Min. Deposit$0
Fractional SharesYes

Charles Schwab is a close second, and for many new investors it may actually be the better choice. The Schwab app is more intuitive, the educational content is more beginner-focused, and the integration between banking and investing is seamless.

Pros

  • Excellent beginner education and onboarding
  • Integrated checking account with ATM fee reimbursement
  • Strong IRA and 401(k) rollover support
  • Physical branches in most major cities

Cons

  • Research tools slightly below Fidelity
  • Options fees slightly higher
Verdict: Ideal if you're new to investing or want to combine banking and brokerage in one place.

Interactive Brokers

★★★★☆
4.6 / 5 — Best for Active Traders
Fees$0–0.005/sh
Min. Deposit$0
Fractional SharesYes

Interactive Brokers is the professional-grade platform that serious traders reach for. The IBKR Pro tier offers access to 150 markets in 33 countries — unmatched for global diversification or international stock picking.

Pros

  • Best international market access
  • Lowest margin rates available
  • Advanced order types and algorithmic trading tools
  • IBKR Lite tier with $0 commissions for casual investors

Cons

  • Steep learning curve — not beginner friendly
  • Interface feels complex and dated
Verdict: Best reserved for experienced traders, options strategists, and investors who need international access.

Robinhood

★★★★☆
4.2 / 5 — Best for Simplicity
Fees$0
Min. Deposit$0
Fractional SharesYes

Robinhood pioneered commission-free trading and remains the go-to app for investors who want a clean, mobile-first experience. Buying your first stock or ETF takes minutes, and the interface stays out of your way.

Robinhood Gold adds margin investing and higher interest on uninvested cash for a flat monthly fee, which can be worthwhile for active users. Crypto trading is built in, unlike most traditional brokers on this list.

Pros

  • Extremely simple, beginner-friendly mobile app
  • Built-in cryptocurrency trading
  • Instant deposits for eligible accounts
  • 24/7 customer chat support

Cons

  • Research and screening tools are basic compared to Fidelity or Schwab
  • No mutual funds or bonds trading
Verdict: A strong pick for first-time investors who want simplicity, but active researchers may outgrow it quickly.

Vanguard

★★★★☆
4.3 / 5 — Best for Long-Term Index Investors
Fees$0
Min. Deposit$0
Fractional SharesLimited

Vanguard invented the low-cost index fund, and its own funds still carry some of the lowest expense ratios in the industry. For buy-and-hold investors building a retirement portfolio around index funds, it's hard to beat on cost.

The trade-off is a platform that feels utilitarian rather than polished. Trade execution and mobile app responsiveness have historically lagged behind Fidelity and Schwab, and the tools skew toward long-term planning rather than active trading.

Pros

  • Industry-leading low-cost index funds and ETFs
  • Strong retirement and target-date fund options
  • Owned by its fund shareholders — aligned incentives

Cons

  • Dated mobile app and web platform
  • Limited fractional share support outside Vanguard funds
  • Not built for active or short-term trading
Verdict: Best suited to hands-off, long-term investors who prioritize low costs over a slick interface.

Webull

★★★★☆
3.9 / 5 — Best for Active Chart Traders
Fees$0
Min. Deposit$0
Fractional SharesYes

Webull targets a similar audience to Robinhood but leans harder into charting and technical analysis, with a desktop-grade platform that active traders tend to prefer. Options and crypto trading are both supported.

Pros

  • Advanced charting tools included at no extra cost
  • Extended-hours trading access
  • Paper trading mode for practicing strategies

Cons

  • Educational content is thinner than Fidelity or Schwab
  • Customer support is slower to respond than larger brokers
Verdict: Worth considering if you actively chart and trade, but not the strongest choice for retirement-focused investors.
